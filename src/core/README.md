# Explaination

Imagine CHIP-8 is a person doing math homework:
Memory is their notebook — 4096 pages, they can write anything on any page
Registers are their fingers — only 16 of them, but super fast to use. Instead of flipping through the notebook they just look at their fingers
Program counter is their finger pointing at the current line of homework. After finishing a problem they move their finger to the next line
Stack is a pile of sticky notes. When they stop their homework to do something else (a function call) they write "come back to line 42" on a sticky note and put it on the pile. When they're done they read the top sticky note and go back
Timers are two egg timers counting down. One controls game speed, one controls the buzzer
Display is a piece of graph paper — 64 squares wide, 32 squares tall. Each square is either filled in black or left white
Keypad is a TV remote with 16 buttons
